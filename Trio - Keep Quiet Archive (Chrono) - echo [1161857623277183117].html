He had lost sense of time again, just like before. How long had he been locked away like this? Days? Months? Years? It felt like eons no matter what was true. The times where he felt human had long vanished. He felt ethereal, angelic, void of feeling. Numb to the outside. He remembered the day The Other came back, he remembered it vividly. He had only met The Other once before in passing, yet he knew that they were always there. They were always where he sat now, watching, waiting in the dark. The false warmth that now ran through his veins was the key to unlocking them. It’s presence was all it took for his guide wires to be severed and handed over to The Other. Staring forward, his only companion in the darkness was an old CRT monitor. On it’s screen were grayscale images of the outside, flickering past like snippets of channels seen through someone’s TV surfing. There was a boy that showed up on it often, one with lighter colored hair and a look of dread. He felt sorry for them, he wanted to apologize to them. He recognized the hands that often stretched out in front of the camera as his own. The Other was hurting the boy. Maybe someday, it would be his turn again. To feel human and see the world in the vibrant colors he remembered. Not this dark, monotone, passenger existence the false warmth forced him into. Maybe then he could save them. He felt like he had to. He still remembered his promise made to the boy on the screen.
